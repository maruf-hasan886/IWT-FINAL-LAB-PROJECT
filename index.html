Mir Mosharraf Hossain Hall is a male residential hall of Jahangirnagar University, established in 1991 and named after the famous Bengali novelist Mir Mosharraf Hossain. It is renowned for its butterfly-shaped modernist design by architect Muzharul Islam, considered one of the finest examples of campus architecture in Bangladesh. The hall covers about 2.81 acres with around 490 rooms in two blocks, housing a large number of students. It has been recognized as one of the top ten contemporary buildings in the country. Currently, Md. Mahmudur Rahman serves as the provost, following a legacy of notable hall leadership. The hall holds cultural, architectural, and historical significance for the university.

Provost: Dr. Md. Rahim Uddin

Established: 1991

Facilities: Library, Canteen, Prayer Room, Wi-Fi, Study Room, Sports Ground, Security

Location: Jahangirnagar University, Savar, Dhaka, Bangladesh
